It goes without saying that chemistry is very important. It covers all areas of our lives. The food we eat, medicine we take, and materials we use every day. It helps us to understand how the world around us works. Getting a chemistry degree is a great investment for your future. There are great options to study chemistry in the Czech Republic. By the way, did you know that one of the Nobel Prize winners in chemistry came from the Czech Republic? Jaroslav Heyrovký discovered the polarography method. Let’s have a closer look at places to study chemistry in the Czech Republic.

University of Chemistry and Technology

If you’re looking for a prestigious university to study chemistry, you can’t go wrong with the University of Chemistry and Technology in Prague. There are many interesting programs you can study there. You can choose from many faculties, e. g. the Faculty of Chemical Engineering, the Faculty of Chemical Technology, or the Faculty of Food and Biochemical Technology. The University of Chemistry and Technology is well known for preparing its graduates for great career options. Students get a lot of practical skills as well as theoretical knowledge necessary to do research in laboratories or to work in fields related to the chemical industry. The university offers many programs and each candidate who wishes to pursue a career in chemistry will surely find something to study.

University of Pardubice

You can also study chemistry at the Faculty of Chemical Technology at the University of Pardubice. Pardubice is a pleasant town well known for horse racing and gingerbread. There is a direct train connection to Prague, which is convenient. This university is an important center for research. There is a wide variety of fields of study, such as analytical chemistry, chemical technologies, biochemical sciences, and much more.

University of South Bohemia in České Budějovice

Another great option outside Prague to study chemistry is the Faculty of Science at the University of South Bohemia in České Budějovice. As its name suggests, this university lies in the south of the country. This university offers a Biological Chemistry Bachelor’s program in English. This program is interesting for its cross-border collaboration with the Johannes Kepler University in Linz, Austria.

Jan Evangelista Purkyně University in Ústí nad Labem

The Faculty of Science at Jan Evangelista Purkyně University in Ústí nad Labem offers a program in English called Computer Modelling in Science and Technology. At this faculty, you can also study Geographies of Transformations in English. This school is worth looking into. Ústí nad Labem is in the north of the Czech Republic. If you like hiking, Bohemian Switzerland is close from there.

Studying chemistry is a great decision to make. Due to the needs of the society we live in, the graduates can expect a bright future. To learn more about the options of studying chemistry in the Czech Republic, click here.
